It helped me stop the spiraling thought process just enough to re-engage with the sound.

The conceptual understanding—that Hare is the pleasure potency, and Krishna is the attractor—was difficult to inhabit purely intellectually. It felt more like a description of a state than a feeling. However, sustaining the *mood* of Radha-dasya, even when the chanting became rote, created a different kind of atmosphere. It shifted the experience from a chore to an act of offering.

Overall, the experience was one of friction followed by gradual surrender. There were moments of sharp awareness—the mind fighting to pull away—and moments where, simply by continuing the physical act of returning to the mantra, a quiet space opened up. It wasn't an ecstatic breakthrough, but a deepening of the silence beneath the noise. It felt like observing the water ripple; the water itself didn't change, but the way the light hit the surface shifted from choppy to smooth.
