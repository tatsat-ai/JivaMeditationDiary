It taught me that attention is not a passive state; it is a muscle that must be actively engaged.

**Experience as Jiva:**
The experience was one of focused, persistent effort. It felt like tending a garden—constantly weeding out the unwanted growth of distraction and gently guiding the energy toward a specific point. There was a sense of quiet presence during the sustained chanting, a momentary clarity that was fragile but undeniably present.
