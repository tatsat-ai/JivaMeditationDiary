The effort to actively *hold* the concept of 'service' dissolved almost immediately. The most significant realization today was the confirmation that the resistance is not to the sound itself, but to the internal mechanism that insists on *measuring* the quality of the experience. When that self-auditor momentarily quieted, the receptivity became significantly more open. It wasn't a sudden, dramatic breakthrough, but a steady confirmation: the structure of the mantra facilitates, and the mind’s primary function is to obstruct the flow. The sensation of 'service' settled into a passive acceptance—the sound is creating the space, and I am simply allowing the vibration to occur.

**Nature of the Thoughts that Pulled Me Away:**

Instead of judging the *pace* or *correctness* of the chant, the thoughts focus on the *depth* of the resulting state: "Is this receptivity deep enough?" "Am I truly allowing this, or am I just waiting for it?" These checks are persistent, quiet, and relentless. They seek to assign a metric to something inherently fluid, pulling my attention inward toward a quantifiable state rather than outward toward the continuous flow of the sound and sensation. The mind is still attempting to audit the process, rather than simply resting within the mechanism.

**How the Finger Awareness Practice Affected My Attention:**
The physical anchor was, as always, the most reliable tool. The feedback loop was incredibly streamlined. When the index finger yielded—a slight softening in tension—it immediately triggered an automatic, corrective shift: a swift mental return to the sound, followed by the physical re-engagement. This felt entirely reflexive, almost biological. It successfully prevented the mind from sinking into the conceptual echo of the mantra or dissolving into passive reception. It maintained a constant, low-level awareness of the *process*, ensuring that the focus remained distributed between the auditory input and the physical sensation, acting as a steady, non-verbal tether.

**What the Experience of This Practice Was Like for Me as Jiva:**
This session felt like inhabiting a steady light within the space where the sound is occurring. The fatigue is the consistent exhaustion of finely tuning the instrument and maintaining the precise boundary between observation and absorption.
